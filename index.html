What Are We Actually Benchmarking in Robot Manipulation?

Tianchong Jiang, Tewodros W. Ayalew, Luzhe Sun, Xiangshan Tan, Samuel Wheeler, Matthew Walter

TTIC, University of Chicago, Argonne National Laboratory

We audit widely used robot manipulation benchmarks with diagnostics for shortcut solvability, statistical significance, creeping overfitting, and data-source dependence.

Diagnostics

Shortcut Solvability

Checks whether benchmark tasks can be solved through exploitable cues instead of intended manipulation behavior.

Statistical Significance

Audits whether reported gains are distinguishable under realistic rollout counts and success-rate variance.

Creeping Overfitting

Tracks whether iterative benchmark use can reward adaptation to benchmark idiosyncrasies over general capability.

Data-Source Dependence

Tests how benchmark outcomes shift when policies draw from different data sources or training mixtures.
